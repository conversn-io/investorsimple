Private Equity

Direct ownership in private enterprises. Long-term capital stewardship beyond public markets.

What Is Private Equity Investing?

Private equity investing involves acquiring ownership stakes in private companies (not publicly traded on stock exchanges) or taking public companies private. Unlike buying stocks on the NYSE, private equity investors directly own and often actively manage businesses, typically holding investments for 5-10 years to implement operational improvements and drive value creation.

Private equity has historically delivered superior returns compared to public markets—top-quartile PE funds have averaged 14-18% annual returns versus 10-11% for the S&P 500 over 20-year periods. The asset class provides access to high-growth companies before they go public, exposure to operational improvements and management expertise, and the illiquidity premium (extra returns for locking up capital). Investors gain from multiple sources: revenue growth, margin improvement, debt paydown, and multiple expansion.

For accredited investors asking "how to invest in private equity", options include direct company investments (typically $100K+ minimums), private equity funds (often $250K-$1M+ minimums), or publicly-traded private equity firms and BDCs (Business Development Companies) accessible with lower capital requirements.

Why Invest in Private Equity?

Superior Historical Returns

Private equity has outperformed public equities over most 10+ year periods. Cambridge Associates reports PE returned 14.3% annually over 20 years versus 9.9% for the Russell 3000. Top-quartile funds often deliver 20%+ IRRs, with exceptional funds reaching 30-40% returns through operational transformation and strategic exits.

Access to High-Growth Private Companies

Private equity provides access to fast-growing companies years before they go public. Many of today's largest companies (Google, Facebook, Uber) generated substantial returns for early PE investors before IPOs. You invest in businesses with proven revenue, not early-stage startups, capturing growth during the most profitable expansion phase.

Operational Value Creation

Unlike passive stock investing, PE firms actively improve companies through strategy, management upgrades, M&A, and operational efficiencies. Portfolio companies receive board-level expertise, capital for growth initiatives, and access to extensive networks. This hands-on approach creates value beyond market appreciation—revenue growth often accelerates 15-25% post-acquisition.

Illiquidity Premium and Tax Efficiency

PE investors earn 2-4% annual illiquidity premium—extra returns for committing capital for 5-10 years. Capital gains treatment applies to successful exits (20% federal rate vs 37% ordinary income). Carried interest structures align fund managers with investor returns, and qualified small business stock (QSBS) can provide tax-free gains on certain investments.

Risk Profile

Strengths

  • Thousands of years of historical value retention
  • Zero counterparty risk when held physically
  • Negative correlation with financial markets during crises
  • Protection against currency debasement
  • Globally liquid and recognized
  • Cannot be printed, diluted, or inflated away

Considerations

  • No yield or cash flow generation
  • Price volatility in short to medium term
  • Storage and security costs for physical holdings
  • Underperformance during strong equity bull markets
  • Potential for government confiscation (historical precedent)
  • Tax treatment may be less favorable than securities

How to Invest in Precious Metals

Physical Ownership

Direct Holdings

Purchase and store physical gold, silver, or platinum in the form of bars, coins, or rounds. Provides maximum control and no counterparty risk.

Options:
  • Gold/silver coins (American Eagle, Canadian Maple Leaf)
  • Bullion bars (various sizes from 1 oz to 400 oz)
  • Home storage or secure vault storage
Best For: Investors prioritizing security and control

Precious Metals IRAs

Tax-Advantaged

Self-directed IRAs that allow physical precious metals holdings with tax benefits. Metals must be stored with approved custodians.

Key Features:
  • Tax-deferred growth (Traditional IRA)
  • Tax-free growth (Roth IRA)
  • Must use approved depositories
Best For: Long-term retirement wealth preservation

Mining Stocks

Equity Exposure

Shares of companies that mine precious metals. Provides leverage to metal prices but introduces company-specific risk.

Considerations:
  • Amplified exposure to metal price movements
  • Operational and management risk
  • Potential for dividends
Best For: Seeking leveraged exposure with equity upside

ETFs & Funds

Paper Exposure

Exchange-traded funds backed by physical metals or mining stocks. Convenient but introduces counterparty risk.

Popular Options:
  • GLD, IAU (gold ETFs)
  • SLV (silver ETF)
  • GDX, GDXJ (mining stock ETFs)
Best For: Liquidity and convenience over physical control

Current Market Context (2026)

Structural inflation and monetary intervention are features of modern systems, not anomalies. Central banks globally continue to expand money supplies while real interest rates remain suppressed. This environment creates asymmetric risk-reward for precious metals:

  • Downside: Limited by production costs and physical demand
  • Upside: Uncapped in response to currency devaluation or systemic stress
  • Cycle Position: Multi-decade debt cycles favor hard assets

Who Should Invest in Precious Metals?

Suitable For

  • Investors seeking wealth preservation over speculation
  • Those concerned about currency debasement
  • Portfolio diversifiers looking for crisis insurance
  • Long-term thinkers with multi-decade horizons
  • Investors who understand volatility is the price of protection
  • Those prioritizing downside protection over yield

Not Suitable For

  • Investors requiring income or dividends
  • Those with short-term time horizons
  • Individuals uncomfortable with price volatility
  • Investors seeking maximum growth potential
  • Those who cannot tolerate underperformance during equity bull markets
  • Investors without proper storage solutions

Key Takeaways

1

Precious metals are insurance, not speculation. They protect wealth rather than maximize returns.

2

Physical ownership eliminates counterparty risk, but introduces storage and security considerations.

3

Typical allocation: 5-15% of portfolio depending on risk tolerance and monetary concerns.

4

Think in decades, not quarters. Precious metals reward patience and punish short-term trading.
